I'm a game developer who fell into infrastructure by way of building the
pipeline tools my team needed. Three years and dozens of shipped systems
later, the infrastructure side is what I love most — the quiet satisfaction
of a deploy that just works, a tool that disappears into the workflow,
a CI run that catches the bug before anyone notices.

My path started in technical art — rigging, shaders, Maya plugins —
and bent toward the systems that hold a studio together: Perforce
servers, build farms, Active Directory, the Bash scripts nobody else
wants to touch. I like the seams of a project, the parts where one
team's work meets another's.

What I'm looking for: a DevOps or tools programmer role where
"engineer who cares about the people using the system" is a feature,
not a quirk. Bonus points if there's a game involved.
